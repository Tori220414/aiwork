Privacy Policy

Aurora Tasks - AI-Powered Task Management

Last Updated: January 12, 2025

Welcome to Aurora Tasks. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information when you use our AI-powered task management application.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account Information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Email address
  • Password (encrypted and securely stored)
  • Display name (optional)
  • Profile information you choose to provide

1.2 Task and Workspace Data

To provide our core service, we collect and store:

  • Tasks you create (title, description, priority, due dates)
  • Workspaces and projects you create
  • Brain dump entries and notes
  • AI-generated plans and suggestions
  • Task completion status and history

1.3 Calendar Integration Data

When you connect calendar services (Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook):

  • OAuth access tokens and refresh tokens (encrypted)
  • Your calendar email address
  • Calendar event IDs for synced tasks
  • Sync status and timestamps

Note: We only access and modify calendar events that you explicitly sync through Aurora Tasks. We do not read or access your other calendar events.

1.4 Payment Information

For subscription management:

  • Stripe customer ID (payment processing handled by Stripe)
  • Subscription status and billing cycle
  • We do NOT store your credit card information - all payment data is securely handled by Stripe

1.5 Usage Data

We collect anonymous analytics data to improve our service:

  • Pages visited and features used
  • Device type and browser information
  • Session duration and interaction patterns
  • Error logs and performance metrics

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 Service Delivery

  • Provide task management and AI planning features
  • Sync tasks with your connected calendars
  • Generate personalized daily and weekly plans
  • Send task reminders and notifications
  • Enable collaboration in shared workspaces

2.2 AI Processing

Your task data may be processed by our AI services (Google Gemini) to:

  • Generate intelligent daily and weekly plans
  • Organize brain dump entries into structured tasks
  • Provide task prioritization suggestions
  • Estimate task durations and complexity

Privacy Protection: AI processing is done securely, and your data is not used to train AI models or shared with third parties for their purposes.

2.3 Service Improvement

  • Analyze usage patterns to improve features
  • Fix bugs and technical issues
  • Develop new features based on user needs
  • Ensure security and prevent fraud

2.4 Communication

  • Send important service updates and announcements
  • Respond to your support requests
  • Send subscription and billing notifications
  • Marketing emails (only if you opt-in, with easy unsubscribe)

3. Data Storage and Security

3.1 Data Storage

Your data is stored securely using:

  • Supabase (PostgreSQL): Primary database for user accounts, tasks, and workspaces
  • Railway: Backend application hosting with encrypted data transmission
  • Vercel: Frontend hosting with SSL/TLS encryption

3.2 Security Measures

  • All data transmitted using HTTPS/TLS encryption
  • Passwords hashed using industry-standard bcrypt
  • OAuth tokens encrypted at rest
  • JWT-based authentication with secure token expiration
  • Regular security updates and vulnerability patches
  • Database access restricted to authorized systems only

3.3 Data Retention

  • Active account data retained as long as your account is active
  • Deleted tasks moved to trash for 30 days before permanent deletion
  • Account deletion: all personal data permanently deleted within 30 days
  • Analytics data anonymized and aggregated

4. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may access your data:

4.1 Essential Services

  • Supabase: Database and authentication - Privacy Policy
  • Stripe: Payment processing - Privacy Policy
  • Google Gemini: AI processing - Privacy Policy

4.2 Calendar Integrations (Optional)

  • Google Calendar API: Calendar sync - Privacy Policy
  • Microsoft Graph API: Outlook calendar sync - Privacy Policy

4.3 Analytics

  • Vercel Analytics: Anonymous usage analytics - Privacy Policy

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

5.1 We DO NOT Sell Your Data

We will never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

5.2 Limited Sharing

We may share your data only in these specific circumstances:

  • With your consent: When you explicitly authorize sharing
  • Service providers: Third parties that help us operate the service (under strict confidentiality agreements)
  • Legal requirements: If required by law, court order, or legal process
  • Safety and security: To protect rights, property, or safety of our users
  • Business transfer: In case of merger, acquisition, or sale (users will be notified)

5.3 Workspace Collaboration

When you share a workspace with other users, they can see:

  • Tasks and projects within that workspace
  • Your name and profile picture (if set)
  • Your activity within the shared workspace

6. Your Rights and Choices

6.1 Access and Control

You have the right to:

  • Access: View all personal data we have about you
  • Correction: Update or correct your information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and data
  • Export: Download your data in a portable format
  • Opt-out: Unsubscribe from marketing emails

6.2 Calendar Permissions

You can disconnect calendar integrations at any time from Settings → Calendar Integration. This will:

  • Revoke Aurora Tasks' access to your calendar
  • Delete stored OAuth tokens
  • Stop syncing new events (existing events remain in your calendar)

6.3 Account Deletion

To delete your account:

  1. Go to Profile → Account Settings
  2. Click "Delete Account"
  3. Confirm deletion

All personal data will be permanently deleted within 30 days. Aggregated, anonymous analytics may be retained.

7.2 Analytics Cookies

We use analytics cookies to understand usage patterns. You can disable these in your browser settings.
